Have you ever wanted to step into a stranger’s half-remembered dream? On a summery Thursday night, I went down to the Vortex theatre to see Cookie Tongue (Omer Gal) and their puppeteering partner Sweet Oddity realize a vision akin to what you might expect from a couple of kids playing make believe in their living room, but with production value and an audience.   


Rather than a story or any kind of structured narrative, we the observers were served a series of ideas and scenes placed in sequence. A driftwood horse marionette floats through the audience and carried a figurine to the stage, where it tries to speak to the tree at the center of the earth. A shadow puppet mimics Cookie Tongue playing the banjo. Another puppet asks the performers to call them a taxi, but when they pick up the phone there’s only birds on the line. A psychic puppet is summoned to tell your fortune but gets distracted wiggling its own eyeballs. This puppet is a landscape – there’s another puppet inside.  

  
Holding everything together conceptually was a backdrop of layered projection screens showing nonstop stop motion animations – people-shaped candles melting and reforming, textures warped and repeated through a tulle curtain.   


Cookie Tongue and Sweet Oddity are a great example of the boundless creativity that is allowed to thrive in Austin. I went into the show not knowing what I was getting into, and I left the show without much more understanding. Regardless, it was a fine way to spend a Thursday night, and I’m glad that shows like this exist.  


Creativity 10/10

Vibes: straddling awe and confusion
